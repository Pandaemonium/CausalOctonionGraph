## 2) Core extraction relevant to COG

1. Particle states are organized as minimal left ideals generated by an idempotent vacuum projector `omega`.
2. Ladder operators (`alpha_j`, `alpha_j^dagger`) build finite basis states over that ideal.
3. The basis is finite and algebraic; this is compatible with COG discrete-state simulation.
4. COG doubled-integer convention (`2*omega`, `2*alpha`) is a faithful execution form over `Z[i]`.

## 3) XOR-notation compatibility

1. Distinct imaginary octonion basis multiplication can be executed by:
   - index channel: `i xor j`,
   - sign channel: Fano orientation sign.
2. This gives a deterministic gate-level implementation of the same left-ideal chain products, provided the same Fano convention is locked.
3. In repo conventions (`rfc/CONVENTIONS.md`), Witt pairs are:
   - `(e6, e1)`, `(e2, e5)`, `(e3, e4)`,
   with `omega = 1/2 (1 + i e7)`.

## 5) Modeling decision for this integration

1. Add **explicit lower-left-ideal motifs** as first-class cycle objects:
   - `S^u` family (from `omega` + raises),
   - `S^d` family (from `omega^dagger` + lowers).
2. Mark motif stability by deterministic e7-cycle period checks (left and right).
3. Keep names conservative (`*_single_*`, `*_double_*`, `*_triple_*`) unless full charge/operator identification is included in the same artifact.

## 6) Notes on interpretation risk

1. Ideal-basis states and SM particle labels are related but not identical unless projector/number-operator readout is declared.
2. In this integration, "stable motif" means deterministic cycle stability under the chosen operator policy, not full phenomenological validation.
