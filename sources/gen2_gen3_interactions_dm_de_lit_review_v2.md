## 1. Scope

This review is focused on:
1. What is experimentally known about second- and third-generation interaction sectors.
2. What the SM predicts for same-generation systems.
3. Whether Gen2/Gen3 sectors are credible direct explanations for dark matter (DM) or dark energy (DE).
4. What this implies for the COG generation-aligned equivalence hypothesis.

## 2. Executive summary

1. SM gauge interactions are generation-universal in form, but observed dynamics are not generation-identical because masses, widths, and mixing differ.
2. Gen2 systems have strong precision anchors (muonium/muonic atoms, charm hadrons), but fully Gen2 stable matter analogs remain experimentally limited.
3. Gen3 systems are precision-rich (bottom sector) but often lifetime-limited for direct atom-like analogs; top decays before ordinary hadronization.
4. There is no established evidence that ordinary Gen2/Gen3 SM particles constitute bulk DM or directly explain DE.
5. Generation-selective BSM portals (especially `L_mu-L_tau`) are active and constrained, so they are useful inspiration but not direct evidence for COG.
6. COG's "same-generation dynamical equivalence" should be treated as a strong, falsifiable new prediction.

## 3. Theory baseline (SM)

## 3.1 Interaction structure

1. Gauge couplings are universal by representation (`SU(3)_c x SU(2)_L x U(1)_Y`).
2. Generation dependence enters through Yukawas (masses) and mixing matrices (CKM/PMNS).
3. Therefore, same-charge systems across generations are not expected to be numerically identical in physical units.

Primary source:
1. PDG Review of Particle Physics (2024): https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.110.030001
2. PDG summary tables portal: https://pdg.lbl.gov/2024/booklet/summary-tables.html

## 3.2 Heavy-flavor dynamics

1. HQET/HQE/NRQCD predict lifetime and spectroscopy differences across heavy-hadron families.
2. Doubly-heavy baryons are sensitive to spectator effects and weak-interference details.

Example source:
1. Doubly charmed baryon lifetime theory: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.98.113005

## 4. Gen2 experimental landscape

## 4.1 Leptonic precision systems

1. Muonic hydrogen is precision established and strongly probes short-distance structure.
2. Muonium spectroscopy has modern high-precision transitions.
3. Muon `g-2` has become an ultra-precise Gen2 electroweak benchmark.

Sources:
1. Muonic hydrogen (Nature 2010): https://doi.org/10.1038/nature09250
2. Muonium `2S-2P` (Nat. Commun. 2022): https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-34672-0
3. Final Muon `g-2` measurement summary article (2025): https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jspc.2025.100233

## 4.2 Charm/charm-rich baryons

1. `Xi_cc++` has been observed and lifetime measured.
2. `Omega_cc+` (`ccs`) remains an active search target in key channels in cited LHCb results.

Sources:
1. `Xi_cc++` observation: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.119.112001
2. `Xi_cc++` lifetime: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.121.052002
3. `Omega_cc+` search channel study: https://doi.org/10.1007/s11433-021-1742-7
4. Updated charm-baryon lifetime precision context: https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP09(2025)157

## 5. Gen3 experimental landscape

## 5.1 Bottom sector

1. Bottomonium and `b`-hadron observables are precisely measured.
2. This is a strong Gen3-quark interaction laboratory, but not "stable atom-like matter" in the ordinary sense.

Source:
1. PDG heavy-hadron sections/tables: https://pdg.lbl.gov/2024/tables/contents_tables_baryons.html

## 5.2 Top sector

1. Top is special: decays on weak timescale before ordinary hadronization.
2. Threshold/quasi-bound effects are studied, but no standard long-lived top-hadron chemistry.

Sources:
1. Top review context: https://doi.org/10.1140/epjc/s10052-012-2120-0
2. Recent toponium-like threshold report context: https://home.cern/news/press-release/physics/elusive-romance-top-quark-pairs-observed-lhc

## 6. DM relevance

## 6.1 What is established

1. Ordinary charged/colored Gen2/Gen3 SM particles are not viable bulk DM candidates.
2. Active neutrinos contribute to hot DM but cannot explain all cold DM.

Sources:
1. Planck 2018 cosmology baseline: https://doi.org/10.1051/0004-6361/201833910
2. DESI BAO cosmology constraints (2025 JCAP, DESI DR1): https://doi.org/10.1088/1475-7516/2025/02/021

## 6.2 Generation-selective portal models

1. `L_mu-L_tau` and flavored-DM frameworks are active BSM lines.
2. They are constrained by precision flavor and neutrino channels (e.g., trident).

Sources:
1. Neutrino trident constraints: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.113.091801
2. `L_mu-L_tau` flavor transitions: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.89.095033
3. Lepton-flavored DM (DMFV example): https://doi.org/10.1007/JHEP05(2023)106

## 7. DE relevance

1. DE evidence is cosmological and currently modeled in expansion-history terms.
2. No accepted experimental chain ties DE directly to Gen2-Gen2 or Gen3-Gen3 microinteraction sectors.
3. DESI-era data motivates dynamical-DE model exploration, but this remains model-dependent.

Sources:
1. DESI BAO baseline cosmology: https://doi.org/10.1088/1475-7516/2025/02/021
2. DESI physics-focused DE classes (PRD 2025): https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.111.023532

## 8. Implications for COG phase-generation hypothesis

Hypothesis being evaluated:
1. Generation-aligned composites (same phase sector, or equally desynced sectors) can show near-equivalent vacuum dynamics.

Literature-consistent interpretation:
1. This is not an SM-established statement.
2. It is a clear BSM/discrete-dynamics prediction and should be pre-registered with explicit pass/fail criteria.
3. The strongest immediate value is internal falsification power, not external confirmation.

## 9. Practical guidance for RFC/test design

1. Use dimensionless observables and matched initial conditions.
2. Separate "vacuum-only equivalence" from "equivalence under non-phase-synced perturbation."
3. Keep a direct SM comparator section in each report, since SM expects mass/width-driven nonequivalence in physical units.
4. Treat Gen3 claims with extra caution due top-lifetime limits and lack of stable Gen3 nuclei analogs.

## 10. Confidence grading

High confidence:
1. SM universality in coupling form + non-universality in mass/mixing.
2. No direct Gen2/Gen3 microscopic explanation of DE in established data.
3. No established bulk DM role for ordinary Gen2/Gen3 charged/colored particles.

Medium confidence:
1. Specific practical transfer from heavy-flavor measurements to COG motif criteria.
2. How far generation-selective portals can constrain COG phase-sector mapping.

Speculative:
1. Direct one-to-one identification between COG phase sectors and SM generations.
2. Exact equivalence of generation-aligned composites at `<1%` for broad observable sets.
