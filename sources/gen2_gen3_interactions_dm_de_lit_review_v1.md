# Gen2-Gen2 and Gen3-Gen3 Interactions: Experimental/Theoretical Literature Review (v1)

Date: 2026-03-02
Owner: COG literature synthesis
Status: Draft (`test_ready` synthesis; no closure claim)

## 1. Scope

This review addresses:
1. What is established experimentally about interactions involving second- and third-generation particles with each other.
2. What the Standard Model (SM) predicts theoretically for same-generation systems.
3. Whether these sectors are plausible direct explanations of dark matter (DM) or dark energy (DE).
4. What this implies for the COG hypothesis that generation-aligned systems can be dynamically equivalent.

## 2. Executive summary

1. In the SM, gauge interactions are generation-universal in form, but masses and mixing are not.
2. Same-generation systems are therefore not expected to be identical across generations in real units; reduced mass, decay width, and hadronization times matter.
3. Stable/long-lived Gen2-Gen2 bound systems exist in useful forms (e.g., muonium/muonic atoms; charm hadrons), but many Gen3 analogs are strongly lifetime-limited (especially top).
4. No established evidence says Gen2/Gen3 SM particles themselves make up cosmic DM; most are unstable or too interacting.
5. No established evidence ties Gen2/Gen3 interaction physics directly to DE.
6. Generation-selective BSM portals (especially `L_mu-L_tau`) are active research lines, but they are model-dependent and constrained.

## 3. What the SM says (theory)

## 3.1 Interaction structure

1. Electroweak and QCD gauge couplings are universal by representation.
2. Generation differences enter through Yukawa couplings (masses) and CKM/PMNS mixing.
3. Therefore, "same charge" does not imply same dynamics in dimensionful observables (radius, period, cross sections), because inertial and decay scales differ.

Core references:
1. PDG 2024 review and tables: https://pdg.lbl.gov/2024/booklet/summary-tables.html
2. PDG review DOI: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.110.030001

## 3.2 Heavy-flavor hadron dynamics

1. HQE/HQET and NRQCD frameworks predict nontrivial lifetime/spectroscopy differences among heavy hadrons.
2. Doubly heavy systems are especially sensitive to spectator effects and interference terms.

Examples:
1. Doubly charmed baryon lifetime theory: https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.98.113005
2. Quarkonium/threshold methods (broad review context): PDG heavy quark sections.

## 4. Experimental status: Gen2-Gen2 sector

## 4.1 Leptonic/exotic-atom systems

1. Muonic hydrogen spectroscopy is precision established and strongly sensitive to short-distance structure.
2. Muonium spectroscopy now has modern precision channels.

References:
1. Pohl et al., Nature 2010 (muonic hydrogen): https://doi.org/10.1038/nature09250
2. Muonium 2S-2P transition (Nat. Commun. 2022): https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-022-34672-0

## 4.2 Charm/charm-containing baryons

1. `Xi_cc++` observed by LHCb (firm experimental milestone).
2. `Xi_cc++` lifetime measured by LHCb.
3. `Omega_cc+` (`ccs`) searched, no significant signal in key LHCb search channel as of that result.

References:
1. Observation `Xi_cc++` (PRL 2017): https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.119.112001
2. `Xi_cc++` lifetime (PRL 2018): https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.121.052002
3. LHCb search `Omega_cc+` (2021): https://doi.org/10.1007/s11433-021-1742-7

Implication:
1. A fully Gen2 +1 baryon analog such as `ccs` is not yet an established stable experimental workhorse like proton.

## 5. Experimental status: Gen3-Gen3 sector

## 5.1 Bottom sector

1. Bottomonium and b-hadron spectroscopy/lifetimes are very well measured.
2. These are strong Gen3-quark laboratories, but they are not stable atomic-matter analogs.

Reference context:
1. PDG bottom hadron sections (2024 tables): https://pdg.lbl.gov/2024/tables/contents_tables_baryons.html

## 5.2 Top sector

1. Top is special: decays on weak timescale before ordinary hadronization in standard treatments.
2. This historically prevented stable top-hadron analogs.
3. Recent collider analyses discuss near-threshold quasi-bound effects ("toponium-like"), but still not normal long-lived hadronic matter.

References:
1. Top review context (EPJC review): https://doi.org/10.1140/epjc/s10052-012-2120-0
2. CERN 2025 update on quasi-bound toponium-like feature: https://home.cern/news/press-release/physics/elusive-romance-top-quark-pairs-observed-lhc

## 6. Dark matter relevance

## 6.1 Established picture

1. Ordinary charged/colored Gen2/Gen3 SM particles are not viable bulk DM (too unstable and/or too strongly interacting electromagnetically/strongly).
2. Active neutrinos contribute a hot component but cannot account for all cold DM.

References:
1. Planck 2018 cosmological parameters: https://doi.org/10.1051/0004-6361/201833910
2. Cosmological neutrino-mass constraint discussions (representative): https://arxiv.org/abs/2007.04994

## 6.2 Generation-selective BSM portals

1. `L_mu-L_tau` models are a major generation-selective pathway in BSM model-building.
2. Strong constraints arise from neutrino trident production and other precision data.

References:
1. Neutrino trident constraint (PRL 2014): https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.113.091801
2. Quark flavor transitions in `L_mu-L_tau` context (PRD 2014): https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevD.89.095033

Interpretation:
1. Gen2/Gen3-selective couplings are theoretically plausible in BSM, but constrained.
2. This supports "possible model space," not direct evidence for your specific mechanism.

## 7. Dark energy relevance

1. Current DE evidence is cosmological (equation-of-state and expansion history) and not tied to observed Gen2/Gen3 interaction sectors in particle data.
2. Dynamical-DE discussions (including DESI-era analyses) do not provide direct generation-sector particle evidence.

References:
1. Planck baseline `Lambda CDM`: https://doi.org/10.1051/0004-6361/201833910
2. Example DESI-era theory fits (model-dependent): https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.15832, https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.02558

Conclusion for this point:
1. No established experimental chain connects Gen2-Gen2 or Gen3-Gen3 microscopic interactions directly to DE.

## 8. Implications for your COG hypothesis

Hypothesis under discussion:
1. If all interacting objects are generation-aligned (or equally desynced), dynamics are identical up to a tight tolerance.

Literature-informed pushback:
1. This is not SM-established physics.
2. It is a strong new prediction that differs from ordinary hydrogenic scaling expectations driven by mass/reduced-mass differences.

What literature does support:
1. Sectorized discrete symmetries and restricted mixing channels are common in model-building.
2. So your hypothesis is plausible as a BSM/discrete-dynamics claim, but must be tested as a prediction.

## 9. Practical recommendations for COG

1. Treat generation-aligned equivalence as a falsifiable contract, not a default assumption.
2. Use model-internal simulations for systems that are experimentally inaccessible (`ccs`-bound analogs).
3. Separate:
   - kinematic equivalence tests in vacuum,
   - perturbation tests with non-phase-synced encounters,
   - ordering/mixing tests.
4. Keep direct comparison to SM expectations explicit in every report.
