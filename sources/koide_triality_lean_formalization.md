## Part A: The Koide Formula

The Koide formula is the key empirical mass-ratio target for COG's tick-frequency mass definition.
It states that for the three charged leptons ($e, \mu, \tau$):

$$Q \;=\; \frac{m_e + m_\mu + m_\tau}{\left(\sqrt{m_e} + \sqrt{m_\mu} + \sqrt{m_\tau}\right)^2} \;=\; \frac{2}{3}$$

This holds to better than $10^{-5}$ precision using pole masses. It is one of the most
precise unexplained numerical relations in the Standard Model.

### K1. Challenge to the Mystery of the Charged Lepton Mass Formula (2005)
- **Author:** Yoshio Koide (original discoverer, 1981)
- **arXiv:** [hep-ph/0506247](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/0506247)
- **Key result:** Reviews why the Koide formula is mysterious (it predicts $m_\tau$ from
  $m_e, m_\mu$ to high precision; it is preserved under radiative corrections). Proposes
  a mechanism based on $\mathbb{Z}_4 \times S_3$ symmetries of the vacuum. Emphasizes that
  the origin is in the **structure of vacuum expectation values**, not in Yukawa couplings.
- **Relevance to COG:** The attribution to VEV structure ↔ COG's claim that mass = tick
  frequency, which depends on the **topological environment** of the node (its graph
  neighborhood), not on an externally fitted Yukawa parameter. The $S_3$ symmetry appearing
  here is the same symmetry generated by $SO(8)$ triality (see Part B).

### K2. Family Gauge Symmetry as an Origin of Koide's Mass Formula (2008)
- **Author:** Yoshio Sumino
- **arXiv:** [0812.2103](https://arxiv.org/abs/0812.2103) (hep-ph)
- **Key result:** Full model: a $U(3) \times O(3)$ family gauge symmetry with 9-component
  scalar field $\Phi$ (taking values in $3 \times 3$ matrices). The $U(3)$ gauge radiative
  corrections **cancel** the QED radiative corrections to Koide's formula, stabilizing it
  against running. The charged lepton spectrum consistent with experiment is reproduced.
  The $U(3)$ family gauge symmetry and $SU(2)_L$ are unified at $10^2$-$10^3$ TeV.
- **Relevance to COG:** Demonstrates that a symmetry mechanism can protect the Koide formula
  against radiative corrections. In COG, the protection mechanism would be different: the
  formula is protected because tick counts are integers (exact discrete quantities), not
  because of gauge cancellations. This is the cleanest physical prediction COG can make:
  mass ratios are exact rational (or algebraic) numbers, not continuously running quantities.

### K3. Another Formula for the Charged Lepton Masses (2017)
- **Author:** Yoshio Koide
- **arXiv:** [1711.03221](https://arxiv.org/abs/1711.03221) (hep-ph)
- **Key result:** Proposes a second lepton mass formula relating the product $\sqrt{m_e m_\mu m_\tau}$
  to $(\sqrt{m_e} + \sqrt{m_\mu} + \sqrt{m_\tau})^3$. Together with the original Koide formula,
  this over-determines the three masses from a single parameter.
- **Relevance to COG:** If both formulas hold, the three lepton masses are determined by
  a single algebraic quantity. This would be a dramatic prediction of COG's mass mechanism
  if it can derive both formulas from the tick-count structure of the three triality images.

### K4. On the Koide-like Relations for the Running Masses (2006)
- **Authors:** Zhi-zhong Xing, He Zhang
- **arXiv:** [hep-ph/0602134](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-ph/0602134)
- **Key result:** The Koide formula is valid at pole masses ($Q^{pole} = 2/3$) but fails for
  running masses at $\mu = M_Z$ (discrepancy ~0.2%). The running is small for leptons but
  large for quarks. Quark-sector Koide-like ratios: $Q_U \approx 0.89$, $Q_D \approx 0.74$.
- **Relevance to COG:** COG's tick-count masses are **infrared** quantities (the asymptotic
  tick frequency of a stable node), so they correspond to pole masses, not running masses.
  The fact that the Koide formula holds for pole masses but not running masses is consistent
  with COG's framework: tick counts are intrinsic, discrete, and do not run.

### K5. What Physics Does The Charged Lepton Mass Relation Tell Us? (2018)
- **Author:** Yoshio Koide
- **arXiv:** [1809.00425](https://arxiv.org/abs/1809.00425) (hep-ph)
- **Key result:** Review of the Sumino mechanism. The exact value $K = 2/3$ is **not** what
  is observed; the observed masses give $K \approx 2/3$ to high precision due to the Sumino
  cancellation of radiative corrections. Reviews related attempts and open problems.
- **Relevance to COG:** Important calibration: the Koide formula is not an exact algebraic
  identity at the QFT level, but a very precise numerical coincidence protected by gauge
  symmetry. COG's derivation would need to explain whether $K = 2/3$ is exact or approximate.

### K6. The Koide Lepton Mass Formula and Geometry of Circles (2012)
- **Author:** Jerzy Kocik
- **arXiv:** [1201.2067](https://arxiv.org/abs/1201.2067) (physics.gen-ph)
- **Key result:** Reinterprets the Koide formula as a statement about the **geometry of
  Apollonius circles** — a generalized Descartes circle formula. The formula becomes:
  the "angle" between three mass vectors (in a specific metric space) is $\pi/4$.
- **Relevance to COG:** The geometric interpretation as a circle-packing condition suggests
  that the Koide formula might arise from a **combinatorial** constraint on how the three
  generation nodes are positioned on the COG graph, rather than from dynamics.
  Specifically, the triality automorphism $V \leftrightarrow S_+ \leftrightarrow S_-$ defines
  a natural 3-fold symmetry, and the Koide condition may be the unique invariant of that
  symmetry in the mass-ratio space.

## Part B: SO(8) Triality and Three Generations

The triality automorphism of $Spin(8)$ ($= D_4$ Dynkin diagram symmetry) is the mathematical
mechanism by which one generation of SM fermions is extended to exactly three generations.

### T1. Exploring Triality Explicitly: Convenient Bases for SO(8), Spin(1,7), and G₂ (2025)
- **Author:** Craig McRae
- **arXiv:** [2502.14016](https://arxiv.org/abs/2502.14016) (math.RT, math-ph)
- **Key result:** Constructs explicit bases for the three 8-dimensional representations of
  $\mathfrak{so}(8,\mathbb{C})$ (the vector $V$ and two spinor $S_\pm$ representations).
  Shows explicitly that triality is simply multiplication of basis elements by **third roots
  of unity** ($\omega = e^{2\pi i/3}$). Shows $\mathfrak{g}_2 \subset \mathfrak{so}(8,\mathbb{R})$
  as the intersection of non-conjugate $\mathfrak{spin}(7)$ subalgebras and as the stabilizer
  of the outer automorphism group. Discusses obstacles to explaining three fermion generations
  via triality.
- **Relevance to COG:** **CRITICAL for `Triality.lean`.** This paper provides the explicit
  matrices needed to implement the triality permutation $V \to S_+ \to S_- \to V$ in Lean.
  The identification of triality with third roots of unity is particularly clean for formalization.
  The discussion of obstacles (the paper is honest that triality alone does not trivially explain
  three generations) sets realistic expectations for COG Phase 4.2.

### T2. The Cl(8) Algebra of Three Fermion Generations with Spin and Full Internal Symmetries (2019)
- **Authors:** Adam Gillard, Niels Gresnigt
- **arXiv:** [1906.05102](https://arxiv.org/abs/1906.05102) (physics.gen-ph, hep-th)
- **Key result:** The minimal left ideals of $\mathbb{C}\ell(8)$ contain three generations of
  SM fermions with full Lorentzian, chiral, weak isospin, spin, and electrocolor degrees of
  freedom. $\mathbb{C}\ell(8)$ unlike $\mathbb{C}\ell(6)$ admits a triality automorphism, and
  it is this triality that extends one generation to exactly three. The triality maps
  $V \leftrightarrow S_+ \leftrightarrow S_-$ are explicitly constructed in $\mathbb{C}\ell(8)$.
- **Relevance to COG:** Establishes that $\mathbb{C}\ell(8) \cong \mathbb{C} \otimes \mathbb{O}
  \otimes \mathbb{H}$ is the correct algebraic home for triality in the SM context. The COG
  graph uses the $\mathbb{C} \otimes \mathbb{O}$ sector for node data; the extension to
  $\mathbb{C}\ell(8)$ by including spin (the $\mathbb{H}$ factor) is COG Phase 4.2.

### T3. Three Generations and a Trio of Trialities (2024)
- **Authors:** Furey, Hughes
- **arXiv:** [2409.17948](https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.17948) (hep-ph, hep-th)
- **Key result:** Identifies $\mathfrak{su}(3) \oplus \mathfrak{su}(2) \oplus \mathfrak{u}(1)$
  within the three triality symmetries $\mathfrak{tri}(\mathbb{C}) \oplus \mathfrak{tri}(\mathbb{H})
  \oplus \mathfrak{tri}(\mathbb{O})$. The triality triple $(\Psi_+, \Psi_-, V)$ with components
  in $\mathbb{C} \otimes \mathbb{H} \otimes \mathbb{O}$ provides all three generations.
  A "Cartan Factorization" merges third-generation spinors into the SM Higgs.
- **Relevance to COG:** The "trio of trialities" $= \text{tri}(\mathbb{C}) \oplus \text{tri}(\mathbb{H})
  \oplus \text{tri}(\mathbb{O})$ is the mathematical structure that COG's `ThreeGenerations.lean`
  and `Triality.lean` must formalize. Each triality factor generates one pair of generation-related
  representations; their product generates all three.

### T4. The Standard Model, The Exceptional Jordan Algebra, and Triality (2020)
- **Author:** Latham Boyle
- **arXiv:** [2006.16265](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.16265) (hep-th, hep-ph)
- **Key result:** One generation is described by the tangent space $(\mathbb{C} \otimes \mathbb{O})^2$
  of the complex octonionic projective plane $(\mathbb{C} \otimes \mathbb{O})P^2$. Three
  generations are related to $SO(8)$ triality. The exceptional Jordan algebra $J_3^8$ encodes
  three generations via its triality structure.
- **Relevance to COG:** Connects the triality mechanism to the exceptional Jordan algebra,
  providing an independent route to three generations. The Jordan algebra may be relevant
  to the COG vacuum structure.

### T5. Outer-Automorphic WZW Orbifolds on so(2n) Including Five Triality Orbifolds on so(8) (2002)
- **Authors:** Ganor, Halpern, Helfgott, Obers
- **arXiv:** [hep-th/0211003](https://arxiv.org/abs/hep-th/0211003)
- **Key result:** Rigorous construction of two $\mathbb{Z}_3$ triality orbifolds and three
  $S_3$ triality orbifolds on $\mathfrak{so}(8)$. Twisted KZ equations for each twisted
  sector. Provides the full algebraic data for triality-twisted conformal field theories.
- **Relevance to COG:** The twisted sectors of the triality orbifold are the algebraic
  analogs of the different fermion generations in COG. The $\mathbb{Z}_3$ orbifold corresponds
  to the cyclic triality $V \to S_+ \to S_- \to V$; the $S_3$ orbifold includes also the
  generation-swapping permutations.

## Part C: Lean 4 Formalization — Physics Infrastructure

### LF1. Formalizing Geometric Algebra in Lean (2021)
- **Authors:** Eric Wieser, Utensil Song
- **arXiv:** [2110.03551](https://arxiv.org/abs/2110.03551) (cs.MS, math.RA)
- **Key result:** Formal Clifford algebra library in Lean/mathlib4. Universal property of
  Clifford algebras, exterior algebras as special case. This is the existing mathlib4
  infrastructure for $\mathbb{C}\ell(6)$, $\mathbb{C}\ell(8)$, $\mathbb{C}\ell(10)$.
- **Practical note:** Available as `Mathlib.LinearAlgebra.CliffordAlgebra` in mathlib4.
  COG can import this for $\mathbb{C}\ell(n)$ construction but must avoid pulling in
  `Analysis.*` or `Topology.*` transitively.

### LF2. The Lean Mathematical Library (2019)
- **Authors:** The mathlib Community
- **arXiv:** [1910.09336](https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.09336) (cs.LO, math.LO)
- **Key result:** Architecture of mathlib: ring theory, module theory, linear algebra,
  group theory, category theory. The foundation for all COG Lean code.

### LF3. Formalization of Physics Index Notation in Lean 4 — PhysLean (2024)
- **Author:** Joseph Tooby-Smith (PhysLean project)
- **arXiv:** [2411.07667](https://arxiv.org/abs/2411.07667) (cs.LO, hep-ph, hep-th)
- **Key result:** First formally verified implementation of Einstein index notation in Lean 4.
  Integrates physics notation with Lean's type system. Uses category theory to handle
  tensor index contractions. Opens a new path for AI-assisted proof of physics theorems.
  The PhysLean library covers Standard Model representations, Lorentz group, gauge fields.
- **Relevance to COG:** **HIGHLY RELEVANT.** PhysLean is the closest existing Lean 4 project
  to COG. Before writing `GaugeGroup.lean`, check if PhysLean already has:
  - $SU(3)$ Gell-Mann matrices
  - $SU(2)$ Pauli matrices
  - $U(1)$ charge operators
  - SM fermion representations
  PhysLean imports: check if they include forbidden `Analysis.*`/`Topology.*` modules.
  If not, COG could import PhysLean as a dependency for SM group theory.
- **GitHub:** Search for "PhysLean" on GitHub — the library is public.

### LF4. Digitalizing Wick's Theorem (2025)
- **Author:** Joseph Tooby-Smith (PhysLean project)
- **arXiv:** [2505.07939](https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.07939) (hep-th, cs.LO)
- **Key result:** Wick's theorem and its static/normal-ordered variants formally proved in
  Lean 4 as part of PhysLean. Demonstrates that key results of perturbative QFT can be
  machine-verified.
- **Relevance to COG:** Demonstrates that Wick's theorem can be formalized — which is
  relevant to the question of how COG's non-associative operator products relate to
  normal-ordered products in QFT. The COG "Batch" operations (where operators are
  associative) should correspond to normal-ordered Wick contractions.

### LF5. LeanExplore: A Search Engine for Lean 4 Declarations (2025)
- **Author:** Justin Asher
- **arXiv:** [2506.11085](https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.11085) (cs.SE, cs.AI)
- **Key result:** Search engine for Lean 4 declarations across Batteries, Init, Lean,
  Mathlib, PhysLean, and Std. Supports semantic search (natural language → Lean declarations),
  BM25+ lexical search, and PageRank-based importance scoring. Available at
  [leanexplore.com](https://www.leanexplore.com/) and via Python API.
- **Relevance to COG:** **Practical tool for daily use.** Before implementing any Lean
  definition, query LeanExplore to check if a similar result already exists in mathlib
  or PhysLean. This prevents reinventing the wheel.
  - Query example: `"octonion multiplication"` → find if anyone has already done this
  - Query example: `"Clifford algebra Fin 7"` → find relevant Clifford algebra constructions
  - Also integrates with LLMs via MCP, useful for proof assistance.

### LF6. A Formal Proof of the Irrationality of ζ(3) in Lean 4 (2025)
- **Authors:** Liu, Zhang, Zhi
- **arXiv:** [2503.07625](https://arxiv.org/abs/2503.07625) (math.NT)
- **Key result:** Formalizes Beukers' proof of irrationality of $\zeta(3)$ in Lean 4.
  Extends mathlib with shifted Legendre polynomials and analytic number theory results.
  First formal proof in Lean 4 of a version of the Prime Number Theorem with error term.
- **Relevance to COG:** Demonstrates the current frontier of Lean 4 formalization capability.
  The COG proofs (Fano plane, octonion alternativity, gauge group emergence) are substantially
  simpler than this, so COG's formalization goals are achievable in the current Lean ecosystem.

## Part D: Related Algebraic Infrastructure

### RI1. Embeddings of the Standard Model in E₈ (2025)
- **Author:** Robert A. Wilson
- **arXiv:** [2507.16517](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.16517) (hep-ph, math.GR)
- **Key result:** Modifies the Manogue-Dray-Wilson "octions" model to restore compactness of
  the SM gauge group. Symmetry breaking of weak $SU(2)$ related to symmetry breaking between
  the three fermion generations. The SM is entirely contained in $\mathfrak{so}(7,3) \subset
  \mathfrak{e}_8$. Mixing angles depend on masses; masses emerge from quantum interactions
  with the dynamic background vacuum.
- **Relevance to COG:** Wilson's $E_8$ embedding is relevant to COG because $E_8$ has
  $G_2 = \text{Aut}(\mathbb{O})$ as a maximal subgroup. The octonionic sector of COG
  lives inside the $G_2$ subgroup of $E_8$. Wilson's explicit embedding gives the group
  theory needed to connect COG's octonionic algebra to the full SM + GUT structure.

### RI2. On the Scales of Masses in Elementary Particles (2011)
- **Authors:** Luis Boya, Cristian Rivera
- **arXiv:** [1104.0795](https://arxiv.org/abs/1104.0795) (hep-ph)
- **Key result:** Attempts to explain the six mass scales of the SM using triality and
  related mathematical arguments. Discusses how triality relates to the three-generation problem.
- **Relevance to COG:** Provides a heuristic framework for the mass hierarchy. The six
  scales (neutrinos, leptons, light quarks, strange, charm, heavy quarks) may correspond
  to six distinct tick-frequency classes in COG, organized by the Clifford algebra grading.
