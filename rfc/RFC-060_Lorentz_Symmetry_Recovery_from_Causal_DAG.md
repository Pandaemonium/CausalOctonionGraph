## 1. Executive Summary

The COG framework is background-independent: it has no background Minkowski
metric. For the theory to agree with experiment, macroscopic observers must
experience approximately Lorentz-invariant physics. This RFC asks: under what
conditions does the COG causal DAG recover approximate Lorentz symmetry?

This is the most fundamental “physics emergence” question in the theory.
Without it, COG cannot make contact with relativistic quantum field theory.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

### 2.1 The causal set precedent

Causal set theory (Bombelli et al. 1987) is the closest existing framework.
A causal set is a discrete partial order. The key theorem:

**Malament (1977):** The causal structure of Minkowski space determines
the topology, differential structure, and metric (up to a conformal factor).

For causal sets: if a causal set is faithfully embeddable in Minkowski space
(in the sense of random Poisson sprinkling), then it inherits approximate
Lorentz symmetry statistically.

**Key open question for COG:** Is the COG causal DAG generated by the update
rule embeddable in (or approximated by) a causal set of this type?

### 2.2 COG-specific complications

Unlike classical causal sets:
- COG nodes carry algebraic state (C⊗O elements), not just a partial order.
- The update rule is deterministic (not random Poisson sprinkling).
- The graph topology changes dynamically (D4 spawn creates new nodes).

This makes Lorentz recovery harder to prove but also potentially richer.

## 3. Proposed Approach

### 3.1 COG lightcone structure (RFC-022)

The COG analog of the lightcone: the set of nodes reachable from node $v$
within $n$ ticks (interaction rounds). Call this the **forward cone** $C^+(v, n)$.

**Conjecture:** For large $n$, the size $|C^+(v, n)|$ grows as $n^3$
(4-dimensional lightcone volume), with corrections of order $n^{-1}$ from
discreteness.

### 3.2 Maximum propagation speed

The update rule propagates information by one edge per tick. This gives a
discrete “speed of light” $c_	ext{COG} = 1$ edge/tick in graph units.

Under the scale calibration (RFC-052), this maps to the physical speed of light.

### 3.3 Lorentz boost as a graph automorphism

A Lorentz boost in continuous spacetime mixes space and time coordinates.
In COG, there is no background space — but there may be an approximate
symmetry of the causal DAG structure at large scales.

**Conjecture:** For COG histories with many nodes, the distribution of
causal link patterns becomes approximately invariant under a discrete
analog of Lorentz boosts.

## 5. Open Questions

1. Does the COG update rule generate causal graphs with effective dimension 3
   (4D spacetime) or some other value?
2. Is there a discrete analog of Malament’s theorem for COG?
3. Can Lorentz boosts be realized as automorphisms of the COG algebraic structure?
4. What are the COG predictions for Lorentz invariance violations at short distances
   (Planck-scale physics)? This would be a falsifiable prediction.
