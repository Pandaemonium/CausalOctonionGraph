## 1. Executive Summary

Gauge anomaly cancellation is a stringent consistency check of the Standard
Model: the chiral fermion content is exactly right for all triangle diagrams
to cancel. This RFC asks whether the analogous property holds for COG's
discrete state space.

**Key numerical finding (calc/anomaly_check.py, 2026-02-26):**

| Coefficient | $Q_{proj}$ (deployed) | $Q_{num}$ (RH sector) | $Q_{num}$ (RH+dual) |
|------------|----------------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| $\mathrm{Tr}[Q]$   | $-0.500$ | $0.000$ | $0.000$ |
| $\mathrm{Tr}[Q^2]$ | $+0.250$ | $+2.667$ | $+5.333$ |
| $\mathrm{Tr}[Q^3]$ | $-0.125$ | $-0.222$ | $0.000$ |

**Conclusion:** $Q_{proj}$ is NOT anomaly-free (it assigns non-zero charge only to
the lepton, not to quarks). $Q_{num}$ (SM electric charge from Witt occupation number)
gives $\mathrm{Tr}[Q_{num}^3] = 0$ only when BOTH the primary Witt ideal (RH sector)
AND its conjugate dual (LH or anti-sector) are included. This matches the standard
SM result exactly.

## 2. Background: Gauge Anomalies

In continuous QFT, a gauge anomaly arises when the fermion path-integral measure
is not invariant under a classical gauge symmetry. The condition for a
$U(1)$ gauge anomaly to cancel over a set of Weyl fermions $\{f_i\}$ is:

$$\mathrm{Tr}[Q^3] = \sum_i Q_i^3 = 0$$

where the sum is over ALL left-chiral Weyl fermions and $Q_i$ is their charge.

The Standard Model satisfies this exactly for each generation of quarks + leptons,
with the cancellation between quarks and leptons being non-trivial.

**Two relevant anomaly conditions:**
1. **Linear anomaly** (gravitational): $\mathrm{Tr}[Q] = 0$
2. **Cubic anomaly** ($U(1)^3$): $\mathrm{Tr}[Q^3] = 0$

## 3. The COG State Space: 8-State Witt Basis

The Witt construction in $\mathbb{C} \otimes \mathbb{O}$ produces a minimal left
ideal generated by the vacuum idempotent $\omega = \frac{1}{2}(1 + ie_7)$.

Applying subsets of the three raising operators
$\{\alpha_1^\dagger, \alpha_2^\dagger, \alpha_3^\dagger\}$ (from CONVENTIONS.md \S5.4)
yields $2^3 = 8$ basis states forming one complete Cl(6) spinor representation:

| $N$ | State | Basis vector (nonzero terms) | SM particle |
|-----|-------|------------------------------|-------------|
| 0 | $\omega$ | $+0.5 e_0 + 0.5i e_7$ | $\nu_R$ (sterile neutrino) |
| 1 | $\alpha_1^\dagger \omega$ | $+0.5i e_1 - 0.5 e_6$ | $u_R$ color r |
| 1 | $\alpha_2^\dagger \omega$ | $-0.5 e_2 + 0.5i e_5$ | $u_R$ color g |
| 1 | $\alpha_3^\dagger \omega$ | $-0.5 e_3 + 0.5i e_4$ | $u_R$ color b |
| 2 | $\alpha_2^\dagger \alpha_3^\dagger \omega$ | $+0.5 e_1 - 0.5i e_6$ | $d_R$ color r |
| 2 | $\alpha_1^\dagger \alpha_3^\dagger \omega$ | $+0.5i e_2 - 0.5 e_5$ | $d_R$ color g |
| 2 | $\alpha_1^\dagger \alpha_2^\dagger \omega$ | $-0.5i e_3 + 0.5 e_4$ | $d_R$ color b |
| 3 | $\alpha_1^\dagger \alpha_2^\dagger \alpha_3^\dagger \omega$ | $-0.5i e_0 - 0.5 e_7$ | $e_R$ (charged lepton) |

Note the crucial structural pattern: states with $N=1$ and $N=2$ live entirely in the
**color subspace** $\{e_1, e_2, e_3, e_4, e_5, e_6\}$. Only the vacuum ($N=0$) and
the lepton ($N=3$) involve the $e_7$ (vacuum axis) direction.

## 4. Charge Operator Analysis

### 4.1 $Q_{proj} = \mathrm{Re}(\psi_7)$ -- the COG deployed operator

From the state table in Section 3, all states with $N = 1$ or $N = 2$ have
**zero** $e_7$ component. Therefore:

$$Q_{proj}(\text{quark state}) = 0 \quad \text{for all 6 quark states}$$

Only the lepton state has a non-zero $e_7$ component:
$$Q_{proj}(e_R) = \mathrm{Re}(-0.5 e_7) = -0.5$$

This makes $Q_{proj}$ a **chirality discriminator** (lepton vs. non-lepton), not the
full electroweak charge. It correctly identifies the lepton but cannot distinguish
quark charges from vacuum.

**Anomaly coefficients for $Q_{proj}$:**
$$\mathrm{Tr}[Q_{proj}] = -0.5 \neq 0 \quad \text{(fails gravitational anomaly)}$$
$$\mathrm{Tr}[Q_{proj}^3] = -0.125 \neq 0 \quad \text{(fails cubic anomaly)}$$

**This is expected, not a bug:** $Q_{proj}$ is the runtime observable deployed for
interaction classification (RFC-041 Layer A). It is not claimed to be the full
gauge charge.

### 4.2 $Q_{num}$ -- Witt number-operator charge

The correct charge operator for anomaly analysis is the **Witt occupation number**
charge, which assigns electric charges based on the number $N$ of creation operators
applied to $\omega$:

$$Q_{num}(\psi) = \begin{cases} 0 & N=0 \text{ (neutrino)} \\ +2/3 & N=1 \text{ (up quark)} \\ -1/3 & N=2 \text{ (down quark)} \\ -1 & N=3 \text{ (charged lepton)} \end{cases}$$

This assignment matches Furey (2016, 2018) and the standard SM right-handed sector.

**Anomaly coefficients for $Q_{num}$ (RH sector alone):**
$$\mathrm{Tr}[Q_{num}] = 0 + 3 \cdot \tfrac{2}{3} + 3 \cdot (-\tfrac{1}{3}) + (-1) = 0$$
$$\mathrm{Tr}[Q_{num}^2] = 0 + 3 \cdot \tfrac{4}{9} + 3 \cdot \tfrac{1}{9} + 1 = \tfrac{8}{3}$$
$$\mathrm{Tr}[Q_{num}^3] = 0 + 3 \cdot \tfrac{8}{27} + 3 \cdot (-\tfrac{1}{27}) + (-1) = -\tfrac{2}{9} \neq 0$$

The RH sector **alone** does NOT cancel the cubic anomaly. This is the correct
behavior: the Standard Model requires both chiralities for full cancellation.

## 5. Full Anomaly Cancellation: Dual Sector Required

The minimal left ideal generated by $\omega$ represents one chirality sector.
The **dual ideal** is generated by $\omega^\dagger = \frac{1}{2}(1 - ie_7)$ and
carries the conjugate fermion content (the LH sector in SM language).

Under charge conjugation, $Q_{num} \to -Q_{num}$, so the dual sector contributes:
$$\mathrm{Tr}[Q_{num}^3]_{\text{dual}} = +\tfrac{2}{9}$$

**Combined anomaly (primary + dual ideals):**
$$\mathrm{Tr}[Q_{num}^3]_{\text{total}} = -\tfrac{2}{9} + \tfrac{2}{9} = 0 \checkmark$$

This is exactly the mechanism Furey (2018) identifies: the two minimal ideals of
$\mathbb{C} \otimes \mathbb{O}$ together form an anomaly-free theory. Neither half
alone is anomaly-free.

**Physical interpretation in COG:**
- The primary Witt ideal: right-handed fermions of one generation
- The dual Witt ideal: left-handed (or anti-) fermions of one generation
- Both are required for a consistent gauge theory

## 6. Implications for COG

### 6.1 Relation between $Q_{proj}$ and $Q_{num}$

$Q_{proj}$ and $Q_{num}$ are related on the lepton states:
$$Q_{proj}(e_R) = -0.5 = \tfrac{1}{2} \cdot Q_{num}(e_R)$$

The scale factor $s = 2$ converts the lepton charge correctly, but fails for quarks
(since $Q_{proj}(\text{quark}) = 0 \neq 2/3$ or $-1/3$).

This confirms RFC-041's analysis: the two operators are distinct, and a bridge on
the full 8-state domain does not exist for $Q_{proj}$. The bridge is valid only
on the lepton subspace (domain restriction $D = \{e_R, \omega\}$).

### 6.2 What is $Q_{proj}$ physically measuring?

$Q_{proj} = \mathrm{Re}(\psi_7)$ measures the **projection onto the vacuum axis**.
States involving $e_7$ are the ones that "remember" the symmetry-breaking direction.

- $\omega$ involves $e_7$ (it IS defined via $e_7$): $Q_{proj}(\omega) = 0$
- $e_R$ involves $e_7$ (it is the conjugate vacuum $-i\omega^\dagger$): $Q_{proj}(e_R) = -0.5$
- Quark states are perpendicular to $e_7$: $Q_{proj} = 0$

$Q_{proj}$ is therefore a **topological charge** distinguishing states by their
$e_7$-sector content, not the full electromagnetic charge.

### 6.3 COG Charge Conservation (the weaker anomaly question)

Even though $Q_{proj}$ does not reproduce SM charges, the COG-native consistency
question (RFC-059 \S3) is: does the D3 gate conserve $Q_{proj}$?

From `calc/charge_sign_interaction_matrix.py` and the locked D3 semantics
(RFC-028), the interaction rule is governed by the sign of $Q_{proj}(\psi)$.
The key question is: is $\sum_i Q_{proj}(v_i)$ conserved under D3 events?

This is a weaker condition than full anomaly cancellation, and it is a
well-defined COG claim to be verified in Lean.

## 8. Decisions to Lock

**D1: Anomaly-free claim scope**
Only the COMBINED (primary + dual ideal) theory is anomaly-free.
The 8-state Witt space alone satisfies $\mathrm{Tr}[Q]=0$ but NOT $\mathrm{Tr}[Q^3]=0$.

**D2: Charge operator for anomaly claims**
All anomaly-related claims use $Q_{num}$, not $Q_{proj}$.
$Q_{proj}$ claims are restricted to runtime interaction classification.

**D3: Dual sector realization**
The dual ideal is realized by applying lowering operators to $\omega^\dagger$.
Its Lean definition is deferred to Phase 2 (depends on RFC-056 for spin content).

---

## 9. Falsification Gates

1. If $\mathrm{Tr}[Q_{num}]$ does not vanish over the 8-state primary Witt space,
   the SM identification is wrong.
2. If $\mathrm{Tr}[Q_{num}^3]_{RH} + \mathrm{Tr}[Q_{num}^3]_{LH} \neq 0$,
   the dual sector cancellation fails.
3. If $Q_{proj}(\text{any quark state}) \neq 0$, the structural analysis is wrong.
4. If the bridge relation $Q_{proj}(e_R) = \frac{1}{2} Q_{num}(e_R)$ fails,
   RFC-041 Layer A and Layer B are more deeply incompatible than assessed.
