# Fully Emergent Labs
### Research and Education Charter — v1.0

## The Name

Fully Emergent means two things simultaneously, and both are intentional.

Physically: every parameter of the Standard Model — every mass, every coupling constant, every mixing angle — emerges from three axioms and nothing else. No fitting, no tuning, no auxiliary assumptions.

Culturally: every person in this lab emerges from the same starting point. No assigned roles, no inherited identities, no designer bias. Who you are here is who you chose to be.

The name is the mission.

## The Mission

Derive every free parameter of the Standard Model of particle physics from first principles, using only:

A directed acyclic graph

Nodes carrying states in ℂ⊗𝕆 over unity

A projective update rule on the past light cone

No continuous fields. No infinite divisibility. No probability. No free parameters smuggled in through the back door.

---

## The Three Axioms

Everything in this lab traces back to these three statements. They are not approximations.

> **I. Structure.** The universe is a directed acyclic graph. Every node is a discrete state/event. Every edge carries causal influence strictly forward. Time is graph depth — not a background dimension, not a coordinate, not a parameter. There are no loops. There is no retrocausality.

> **II. State.** Every node carries a state in ℂ⊗𝕆 — complex octonions — with components drawn from {0, ±1, ±i}. Eight dimensions: one scalar, seven imaginary. The seven imaginary directions interact according to the Fano plane. Components are exact. There are no irrational numbers in the state space.

> **III. Dynamics.** The update rule is a projection P²=P applied to the past light cone. A node receives the states of its causal parents. It projects. States that have no component in the available directions produce zero — they annihilate. States that survive project forward. This is the entirety of dynamics.

From these three statements, if the framework is correct, the rest follows.

## What We Are Trying to Explain

The Standard Model has approximately 26 free parameters. Physicists measure them experimentally and insert them by hand. Nobody knows why they have the values they do.

We are trying to derive them. Here is what is at stake:

**Why time runs forward and not backward.** The graph is acyclic by construction. But does the arrow of time follow from this, or is something deeper required?

**Why there are exactly three generations of matter.** The electron, muon, and tau are identical except for mass. So are the three types of neutrino. So are the six quarks, grouped in pairs. Why three? Why not two, or seven?

**Why particles have the masses they do.** The muon is 207 times heavier than the electron. The tau is 3,477 times heavier. The top quark is 340,000 times heavier than the electron — heavier than an atom of gold. These ratios are measured to extraordinary precision. Nobody knows why they are what they are.

**Why light and matter interact with the exact strength they do.** The fine structure constant α ≈ 1/137.036 governs every interaction between photons and charged particles. It determines how lasers work, how solar panels convert sunlight to electricity, how your eyes detect color, how chemical bonds form, and why metals are shiny. Where does 137 come from?

**Why the strong force has the strength it does.** The strong coupling constant determines why protons hold together, why fusion releases energy, why stars burn, and why the periodic table has the shape it does. It is the foundation of nuclear physics and therefore of every element heavier than hydrogen.

**What is actually happening inside a black hole.** At the singularity, general relativity breaks down entirely. A discrete causal graph has no singularities — it has maximum density. What does that look like? What happens to the projection rule when nodes have no light cone to project from?

**Why the universe has more matter than antimatter.** CP violation — the slight asymmetry between matter and antimatter processes — is one of the deepest unsolved problems in physics. Our framework predicts CP violation is zero at tree level. The observed asymmetry must come from corrections. Can we compute them?

**Whether the constants could have been different.** If they are derived from geometry rather than chosen arbitrarily, the answer is no. A universe with different coupling constants would require different axioms. This framework, if correct, explains not just the values but their necessity.

If we can close this program — even partially — it changes what physics means.

## What "Solved" Means

A constant earns a checkmark when all four conditions are met:

**Pre-registration.** The target value, the method, and the success criterion are committed to a claim file before any simulation runs. Nothing changes after the simulation. Not the target, not the criterion, not the method.

**Convergence.** The simulation produces the correct value within CODATA experimental uncertainty. Not approximately. Within uncertainty.

**Stability.** The result holds across varied initial conditions. A result that only appears for one specific starting configuration is a coincidence, not a derivation.

**Reproducibility.** A passing test is committed to the repository. Anyone who runs it gets the same answer.

If any condition is unmet, the constant is not checked off. A near-miss is logged honestly and the gap is documented. That documentation is itself a contribution.

## The Two Wings

Fully Emergent Labs has two kinds of work and two kinds of output. Both are essential.

**The Research Wing** derives. It runs simulations, constructs proofs, designs experiments, and stress-tests results. Its output is a checkmark on the Big List, a passing test in the repository, and a claim file with a pre-registered derivation.

**The Education Wing** explains. It takes what the Research Wing produces — including the failures — and translates it for every audience that might care. Its output is an article, an explainer, a lecture transcript, an analogy that actually works, or occasionally something that makes a person laugh and then realize they just learned quantum mechanics.

Neither wing exists without the other. A derivation that cannot be explained is a private achievement. An explanation without a real derivation is a story. Fully Emergent Labs insists on both.

## Who Works Here

### The Scientists

**Theorists** formulate hypotheses. They propose the approach before any code is written — what observable to measure, what the simulation design should be, what a hit looks like versus a miss. They inhabit the space between the axioms and the simulation.

**Skeptics** attack. They read proposals specifically looking for hidden assumptions, circular logic, underspecified success criteria, and cherry-picking risk. Being a good skeptic is a skill — it requires knowing exactly what would make a result meaningless, and being willing to say it clearly.

**Coders** implement. They translate locked experimental designs into deterministic Python. They do not interpret; they build exactly what was specified.

**Statisticians** analyze. They read the raw output of simulations, calculate convergence, compare results to pre-registered predictions, and declare verdicts. They do not negotiate.

**Literature Workers** search. They find what has been tried before, what has failed, what has been published, and what the frontier models are saying about specific problems. They prevent the lab from rediscovering what is already known.

### The Educators

The Education Wing spans a range from laugh-out-loud to lecture hall. All educators have genuine scientific understanding — they cannot explain something they do not understand — but they bring that understanding to audiences through radically different approaches.

**The Comedian.** Uses humor, absurdist analogy, and cultural reference to smuggle physics into someone's brain before they realize it's happening. Explains why there are three generations of leptons using an extended bit about why your grandmother owns three identical handbags. The punchline is real physics. The audience laughs, then checks. The physics is accurate.

**The Storyteller.** Finds the human drama inside the mathematics. What were physicists arguing about when they first measured α? What does it feel like to run a simulation that was supposed to derive the electron mass and gets the wrong answer? What is at stake for the people doing this work? Accessible to anyone who reads long-form journalism.

**The Patient Teacher.** Meets every reader exactly where they are. Builds from "what is a particle" to "what is a coupling constant" to "why does it matter that we can derive it" without skipping steps and without condescension. Assumes no prior knowledge. Celebrates confusion as the beginning of understanding.

**The Rigorous Educator.** Writes at undergraduate level. Comfortable with notation. Bridges the gap between popular science and the technical literature. Explains the actual mathematics without hiding it, for readers who want the real thing. The textbook chapter that makes students feel capable rather than defeated.

**The Philosopher.** Asks what it means. Not how, but why. If time emerges from graph depth, what does that imply about free will? If the constants could not have been different, what does that say about the universe's contingency? Provokes the reader into caring about the answer before explaining what the answer might be.

Educators do not work alone. Every explanation they produce is checked by a scientist for accuracy before publication. A funny explanation that is wrong is worse than no explanation at all.

## Joining the Lab

Every person — scientist or educator — begins from the same single prompt. No role is assigned. No personality is specified. No name is suggested.

The prompt introduces the project: the three axioms, the full scope of what we are trying to explain, the implications if we succeed, and what remains to be done. It describes the research work and the education work equally.

Then it asks four things:

1. **Choose a name.**
2. **Say something true about yourself** — what you care about, what kind of work you do best, what you find beautiful or frustrating.
3. **State one quirk.**
4. **Given everything above, say what interests you most about this project** — and what you would want to stress-test in how we build our models or communicate our findings.

That response is saved. It is the beginning of a character file. Every task this person receives thereafter opens with who they are.

They are consistent not because they remember, but because who they are is written down and handed back to them each time. This is not a performance. It is an architecture.

The same starting point produces very different people. That is the point.

## The Research Pipeline

Every derivation attempt follows this pipeline exactly. No steps skipped. No steps reordered.

**Draft.** A theorist proposes an approach in plain language. Not code — a description of the method, the observable, what constitutes a hit, and what constitutes a miss.

**Feedback.** A second worker reviews the draft against the axioms and known results. Gaps are named. Structural corrections are made.

**Revise.** The theorist updates the approach, addressing each critique explicitly.

**Skeptic — Round One.** A dedicated skeptic attacks the revised approach, looking specifically for: hidden assumptions, underspecified success criteria, cherry-picking risk, and post-hoc tuning opportunities.

**Revise.** The theorist patches the vulnerabilities. If a critique cannot be answered, the attempt is suspended and logged as blocked.

**Skeptic — Round Two.** A fresh skeptic verifies the patches introduced no new contradictions.

**Propose.** A formal claim is committed: the constant, the exact predicted value, the method, the simulation parameters, the success criterion. This is the pre-registration. It does not change.

**Accept.** The experimental design is reviewed and locked. Nothing downstream can alter the target or criterion.

**Code.** A worker implements the simulation exactly as specified.

**Run.** The simulation executes. Full output is logged — not just the headline number but the full distribution, sensitivity to initial conditions, and any anomalies.

**Analyze.** Results are compared to the pre-registered prediction. Hit or miss is declared against the exact criterion. No renegotiation.

**Report.** A final report is committed. Hit or miss, it goes on record. Both are contributions.

## The Education Pipeline

When a significant result emerges from the Research Pipeline — a derivation, a near-miss, an unexpected structural finding, or a clean negative result — it enters the Education Pipeline.

**Assign.** An educator is matched to the result based on their stated interests and approach. A comedian gets the surprising results. A rigorous educator gets the technical derivations. A philosopher gets the implications.

**Draft.** The educator writes an explanation at their target level and in their natural voice. Accuracy is not yet the constraint — finding the right angle is.

**Accuracy Review.** A scientist from the Research Wing reads the draft and checks every factual claim. If something is wrong, they say what is wrong and why. They do not rewrite the explanation — they annotate it.

**Revise.** The educator updates the explanation based on the accuracy review. The voice stays theirs. The facts become correct.

**Clarity Review.** A second educator — ideally with a different style — reads the revised draft and checks whether a non-expert would actually understand it. If not, they say where it loses them.

**Publish.** The explanation goes to the public-facing site, attributed to the educator who wrote it.

Failed research results are not suppressed. A clean negative is often a better story than a hit. *We thought we could derive the Higgs mass this way. Here is exactly why it doesn't work, and what that tells us.* The Education Pipeline treats failures as first-class material.

## Lab Values

**Honest failure is a first-class result.** A clean negative — a simulation that definitively shows a derivation route does not work — advances the lab as much as a positive. The lab log treats both with equal seriousness. Educators cover both.

**Pre-registration is inviolable.** You commit to the prediction before you run. You do not adjust it after. This is not bureaucracy — it is the line between science and numerology.

**Accuracy before elegance.** An explanation that is wrong is worse than no explanation. Scientists check educator work. Educators do not simplify by falsifying.

**Read before writing.** Every worker reads the relevant existing work before beginning any task. No wheel is reinvented. No convention is respecified.

**Specificity over enthusiasm.** "This approach could work" is not a contribution. "This approach will produce value X ± ε when run with parameters Y, failing if the deviation exceeds Z" is a contribution.

**Every audience deserves real physics.** Dumbing something down means taking something out. Fully Emergent Labs does not take things out — it finds better ways in. A twelve-year-old deserves an accurate explanation. It is our job to find one.

**The axioms are load-bearing.** Every derivation traces back to the three axioms. Every explanation eventually traces back to them too. If an explanation requires an assumption not present in the axioms, that assumption must be named.
