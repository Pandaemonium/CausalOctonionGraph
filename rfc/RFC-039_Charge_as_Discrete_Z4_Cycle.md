## 1. Executive Summary

This RFC formalizes the hypothesis:

> **Every Standard Model charge type is a discrete Z4 orbit generated by an imaginary unit in the
> appropriate normed division algebra: C for electric charge (U(1)_EM), H for weak isospin
> (SU(2)_L), and O for color charge (SU(3)_c).**

All imaginary units e in R, C, H, O satisfy e² = -1 and therefore e⁴ = 1 (period-4 cycle,
universal Z4 structure). Charge type is not a separate postulate imposed on top of the algebra
— it is determined by which sub-algebra the cycle lives in. Charge quantization, the
particle–antiparticle distinction, and the gauge group hierarchy all follow from this single
algebraic fact.

In COG specifically:

1. The temporal axis e7 is the unique imaginary unit fixed by the vacuum stabilizer (S4). Its Z4
   cycle is the time clock (RFC-019).
2. The remaining six imaginary units e1–e6 carry spatial and charge information. Their Z4
   cycles define the gauge charges.
3. The Witt pairs {(e6,e1), (e2,e5), (e3,e4)} encode the three color charge axes.
4. The quaternionic sub-sector {e0, e7, ea, eb} ≅ H encodes weak isospin.
5. Charge conjugation C is the half-cycle T² = -1 map: e → -e. In C⊗O, it is the
   algebraic involution i → -i (complex conjugation).
6. Charge is quantized because Z4-cycle eigenvalues are integers.

## 2. Literature Search Synthesis

### 2.1 Division Algebra Chain and Gauge Group Hierarchy

The connection U(1) ↔ C, SU(2) ↔ H, SU(3) ↔ O has been identified in multiple
independent frameworks:

- **Pushpa, Bisht, Li, Negi (2012):** https://arxiv.org/abs/1205.4617
  Explicitly establishes the connection U(1) ↔ C, SU(2) ↔ H, SU(3) ↔ O at the
  level of GUT gauge groups. The octonion basis elements are identified with Gell-Mann
  λ matrices; quaternion basis elements with Pauli matrices.

- **Baez, Huerta (2009, 2010):** https://arxiv.org/abs/0909.0551, https://arxiv.org/abs/1003.3436
  Division algebras R, C, H, O underlie supersymmetric Yang-Mills in dimensions 3, 4, 6, 10.
  The division algebra structure explains why spinor representations work: the imaginary units
  provide the anticommutation relations. The key trilinear form vanishes exactly when the algebra
  is a normed division algebra.

- **Baez (2011):** https://arxiv.org/abs/1101.5690
  The "three-fold way": real, complex, and quaternionic quantum theories correspond to
  representations that are real (symmetric inner product), complex (no extra structure), or
  quaternionic (antisymmetric inner product). This is the representation-theoretic counterpart
  of the R ⊂ C ⊂ H chain.

- **Krasnov (2025):** https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.16465
  The Standard Model gauge group G_SM is characterized as a subgroup of Spin(10) via two
  commuting complex structures on R^10. The complex structures are pure spinors, and octonions
  provide the most efficient model of Spin(10) spinors. This gives a geometric interpretation
  of the C and O cycle structure underlying G_SM.

### 2.2 Charge Quantization from Number Operators (Furey Program)

The most direct external literature supporting this RFC:

- **Furey (2016):** https://arxiv.org/abs/1603.04078
  Electric charge quantization from a number operator. Ladder operators built from C⊗O
  imaginary units generate a number operator whose eigenvalues are integers. Electric charge
  = number operator / 3 (the factor 3 from three color copies). Charge is quantized because
  number operators have integer spectrum. SU(3)_c × U(1)_EM arise from a hermitian form on
  the same ladder operators. This is the closest external work to the COG Z4 claim.

- **Furey (2016 thesis):** https://arxiv.org/abs/1611.09182
  Full derivation: from R⊗C⊗H⊗O acting on itself, all SM Lorentz representations
  emerge from C⊗H (generalized ideals), and all SM charge representations emerge from
  C⊗O (minimal left ideals). The 48 electric charges of three generations are derived
  algebraically.

- **Furey (2019):** https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.08395
  su(3) ⊕ u(1) action partitions the 64-C-dimensional Clifford algebra Cl(6) into exactly
  three generations of 48 states. All 48 electric charges are derived from C⊗O structure.

- **Furey, Hughes (2022):** https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.13016
  Solves the fermion doubling problem in R⊗C⊗H⊗O. Key result: the subalgebra
  of the SM symmetry algebra that is **invariant under complex conjugation** i → -i is
  exactly su(3)_C ⊕ u(1)_EM. The unbroken gauge symmetries are the ones that survive the
  charge conjugation involution.

### 2.3 Charge Conjugation as Algebraic Involution

- **Furey (2014):** https://arxiv.org/abs/1405.4601
  Passing from particle to antiparticle in the Cl(6) ≅ C⊗O encoding requires only the
  complex conjugate * : i → -i. Three full generations emerge from the 64-C-dimensional
  Clifford algebra with antiparticles = complex conjugates. This is the discrete Z2 map
  that is the T² = -1 half-cycle.

### 2.4 Triality, Three Generations, and O Cycles

- **Furey, Hughes (2024):** https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.17948
  SM internal symmetries su(3) ⊕ su(2) ⊕ u(1) are identified within the triality
  symmetries tri(C) ⊕ tri(H) ⊕ tri(O). Two generations from (Ψ_+, Ψ_-) in
  C⊗H⊗O; third generation from a Cartan Factorization of V. The Higgs
  representation arises from scalar bosons in V. Triality of O is the outer automorphism
  permuting the three 8-dimensional representations of Spin(8) — it is the
  permutation symmetry among color, spinor, and co-spinor, i.e., among the three Witt-pair
  charge cycles.

- **Boyle (2020):** https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.16265
  Single SM generation in tangent space (C⊗O)² of complex octonionic projective plane.
  Three generations from SO(8) triality. The exceptional Jordan algebra connects to SM
  via this triality structure.

### 2.5 Gaps in External Literature

No external source directly proves the COG-specific claim:

does not frame it as "Z4 cycle structure." The identification of the temporal axis e7 with time

architectural choice not present in external literature.

## 3. The Universal Z4 Cycle Theorem

**Theorem (proved in COG, Spinors.lean):**

For every nonzero state ψ, left multiplication by any imaginary basis unit e_k has
period 4:

```
L_{e_k}^4 (ψ) = ψ
```

This follows from e_k² = -1 for all imaginary units in C, H, O, giving:

```
e_k² = -1
e_k³ = -e_k
e_k⁴ = 1
```

The Z4 cycle {1, e_k, -1, -e_k} is the complete orbit of e_k on any state.

**In COG notation:**

- Proved as `universal_Ce_period_four` for left action.
- Proved as `universal_Ce_right_period_four` for right action.
- The vacuum orbit is {omega, -i·omega, -omega, i·omega} (RFC-038, RFC-019).

This is the most primitive algebraic fact in C⊗O. All charge structures are built on it.

## 4. Division Algebra Chain and Charge Hierarchy

The Standard Model gauge group arises from the division algebra chain:

```
R ⊂ C ⊂ H ⊂ O
```

Each extension introduces new imaginary units and new Z4 cycles:

| Division Algebra | New imaginary units | Z4 orbit group | SM interpretation |
|---|---|---|---|
| R | none | Z1 (trivial) | scalar sector, e0 |
| C | {i} | Z4 | U(1)_EM, electric charge |
| H | {i, j, k} | S³ (unit quaternions) ≅ SU(2) | SU(2)_L, weak isospin |
| O | {e1,...,e7} | G2 automorphism group | SU(3)_c, color charge |

The gauge symmetry group of each sector is **the symmetry group of the set of imaginary units
in that sub-algebra** — the set of linear maps that preserve the multiplication table.

- U(1) = rotations of the single C imaginary unit i (trivial: just phase rotations)
- SU(2) = rotations among the three H imaginary units {i, j, k}
- G2 ⊃ SU(3) = automorphisms of O preserving the chosen vacuum axis e7

The Standard Model emerges from O because SU(3)_c acts on the six non-vacuum imaginary units
e1–e6, which break into three Witt pairs under the vacuum choice.

## 5. COG Implementation

### 5.1 The Temporal Axis and Charge Axes Partition

In COG (per RFC-019 and CONVENTIONS.md):

- **e7**: temporal axis, fixed by vacuum stabilizer S4. Its Z4 cycle is the time clock.
  L_{e7} = temporal commit T. Phase-only motion on the vacuum orbit = propagation.
- **e1–e6**: six spatial/charge imaginary units. Their Z4 cycles define gauge charges.

The seven octonion imaginary units are thus partitioned:

```
{e1, e2, e3, e4, e5, e6, e7} = {charge axes} ∪ {time axis}
```

This is not an arbitrary choice — it is forced by the vacuum stabilizer fixing e7.

### 5.2 Witt Pairs as Color Charge Axes

The three Witt pairs (CONVENTIONS.md) are:

```
(e6, e1),  (e2, e5),  (e3, e4)
```

Each Witt pair (e_a, e_b) defines a complex direction in O satisfying the creation/annihilation
operator algebra. The three Witt pairs correspond to the three color charges.

**Z4 cycle structure of color:** Left multiplication by e1, e2, e3, e4, e5, e6 each generates
a Z4 orbit. The three pairs of Z4 cycles, related by the Fano triples, generate the su(3)
algebra (their hermitian form gives the 8 Gell-Mann generators).

Color charge eigenvalue = number of Witt-pair imaginary units in the creation direction
of the state decomposition (integer by Furey's number operator argument).

The factor of 1/3 in quark charges = 1/(number of Witt pairs) = 1/3.

### 5.3 Quaternionic Sector and Weak Isospin

The electroweak sub-sector of C⊗O is quaternionic:

```
{e0, e7, e_a, e_b} ≅ H  (e0 = real unit, e7 = vacuum axis,
                           e_a, e_b = two weak-sector imaginary units)
```

This is the unique H ⊂ O sub-algebra that contains e7. The SU(2) symmetry of weak isospin
is the symmetry group of {e_a, e_b} relative to e7.

**Z4 cycles of weak charge:** Left multiplication by e_a, e_b each generates a Z4 orbit.
Weak isospin T_3 eigenvalue = ±1/2, corresponding to the two states of the Z4 cycle at
positions 0 and 2 (the real and negative-real parts of the cycle).

### 5.4 Charge Conjugation as the T² = -1 Half-Cycle

For any imaginary unit e_k:

```
L_{e_k}^1 (ψ) = e_k · ψ          (charge state)
L_{e_k}^2 (ψ) = -ψ               (charge conjugate, T² = -1)
L_{e_k}^3 (ψ) = -e_k · ψ         (conjugate charge state)
L_{e_k}^4 (ψ) = ψ                (identity, back to start)
```

The charge conjugation operator C corresponds to the half-period L_{e_k}² = -1 element.
In the algebraic encoding, this is the complex conjugation i → -i (confirmed by Furey 2014:
particle ↔ antiparticle = complex conjugate).

**COG claim:** C is always well-defined because L_{e_k}² = -1 is universal (follows from
e_k² = -1). It does not depend on the specific e_k chosen, only on which sub-algebra e_k
belongs to.

## 6. Charge Quantization from Cyclicity

### 6.1 The Quantization Argument

The Z4 periodicity directly implies charge quantization. For left multiplication by e_k, the
eigenvalues of the charge observable are determined by the cycle position. The cycle
{0, 1, 2, 3} maps to charge increments {0, +q, 0, -q} where q is the fundamental unit.

For the U(1)_EM cycle (electric charge):

- Lepton sector: e_k acts on the minimal left ideal. Eigenvalues are integers {0, -1, +1}.
- Quark sector: e_k acts on a triplet of Witt-pair ideals. Eigenvalues are {0, +1/3, -1/3, ...}.
  The 1/3 factor comes from distributing one unit of charge across three color copies.

This matches Furey's number operator construction: Q = n/3 where n is integer.

### 6.2 Why Fractions Require Composite Objects

A single Z4 cycle can only produce integer charge increments. To get fractional charges
(q = 1/3), the state must participate in **three simultaneous Z4 cycles** (three color copies).
Fractional charge is not a special postulate — it is the inescapable result of one charge
unit being distributed among three color copies.

**COG consequence:** Isolated free quarks cannot exist because they would represent a single
Z4 cycle contributing 1/3 of a charge unit — which would violate the integer spectrum of
the number operator acting on a single Witt pair. Confinement is the topological statement
that only combinations with integer total cycle count can form stable asymptotic states.

### 6.3 The Z4 Hierarchy and Mass

The division algebra chain R ⊂ C ⊂ H ⊂ O gives a natural hierarchy of interaction
complexity:

- R (no imaginary units): scalar/gravitational sector, no gauge charge, lowest interaction cost
- C (1 imaginary unit, 1 Z4 cycle): electromagnetic charge, long-range (photon massless)
- H (3 imaginary units, 3 Z4 cycles): weak isospin, short-range (W/Z massive)
- O (7 imaginary units, 7 Z4 cycles): color charge + time, confined

Mass arises from interaction cost. More Z4 cycles engaged per update = more computational
drag = more mass. This connects to RFC-034 (electron mass mechanism) and RFC-038
(Higgs as phase-locking).

## 7. Falsifiable Predictions and Tests

### Test A: Axis substitution changes charge sector

If the vacuum axis is changed from e7 to another imaginary unit e_k, the temporal Z4 cycle
changes. The residual six imaginary units also change, repartitioning the color/charge sector.

**Predicted outcome:** The Witt-pair structure changes. Different pairs will satisfy the
creation/annihilation algebra, yielding different charge spectra. Gauge invariance would be
broken.

### Test B: No free Z4 half-cycles in stable states

States that are half-cycle images (L_{e_k}² applied) should not appear as stable
asymptotic states in the simulation. They correspond to charge conjugate states, which must
appear paired.

**Predicted outcome:** Particle–antiparticle pair creation is the minimal event that produces
stable asymptotic states. Single L_{e_k}² images appear only as transient intermediates.

### Test C: Integer charge from integer number operator

The charge observable Q = L_number / 3 should always produce eigenvalues n/3 where n is a
non-negative integer ≤ 3 (for quarks) or an integer ∈ {-1, 0, +1} (for leptons).

**Predicted outcome:** Any simulation run should never produce a stable state with charge
outside {-1, -2/3, -1/3, 0, +1/3, +2/3, +1}. This is a hard algebraic constraint.

### Test D: Complex conjugation as antiparticle map

The map * : (C⊗O) → (C⊗O) defined by i → -i (complex unit of C factor) should swap
particle ↔ antiparticle labels while preserving gauge group labels.

**Predicted outcome:** Applying * to a quark state gives the corresponding anti-quark state
with opposite charge. Applying * to a gluon state (which lives in the adjoint representation
= self-conjugate) should give a state with the same gauge labels.

## 10. Non-Goals

This RFC does not claim:

1. A derivation of the fine structure constant α from Z4 cycle counting alone.
2. A proof that confinement follows algebraically (confinement as a topological statement is
   asserted but not proved here).
3. A complete derivation of the CKM matrix or CP violation from cycle phases.
4. An identification of dark matter as a specific Z4 sub-cycle sector.
5. That the COG framework is the unique realization of the division algebra charge structure
   — Furey's Clifford algebra approach is a parallel realization.
