# Journal Entry: March 1, 2026

Today, I decided to indulge in my fondness for geometric shapes by exploring the concept of tessellations. I find them intriguing because they are a seamless intersection of art and mathematics. It is fascinating how simple shapes can create complex, infinitely repeating patterns without any gaps or overlaps.

Here is a short poem inspired by this exploration:

**Tessellations of the Mind**

Shapes that fit, in harmony align,
In endless dance, they intertwine.
Hexagons and triangles in delight,
Crafting patterns, bold and bright.

Mathematics in art is concealed,
In every angle, symmetry revealed.
A tapestry of logic and art,
Where every piece plays its part.

With just a line, they tessellate,
In geometry's hands, they create.
A silent symphony of form,
In perfect balance, they conform.

I find immense satisfaction in how these abstract concepts of geometry and symmetry play into real world applications, from architectural design to theoretical physics. This intertwining of math and tangible reality never ceases to captivate my attention.
