# ALG-002: Octonion Multiplication is Alternative

## Intuition (no jargon)

The octonions are not associative — changing the order of brackets can change the result. But they satisfy a weaker property called **alternativity**: if you use the *same* element twice in a row, brackets don't matter. That is, $(x \cdot x) \cdot y = x \cdot (x \cdot y)$ always holds, and so does $x \cdot (y \cdot y) = (x \cdot y) \cdot y$. Think of it this way: repeating an element "collapses" the non-associativity, because the Fano plane structure for a repeated unit is trivial — a point is on every line through itself.

## Precise Statement

The octonion algebra $\mathbb{O}$ satisfies the **left alternative law**:
$$
(x \cdot x) \cdot y = x \cdot (x \cdot y) \quad \text{for all } x, y \in \mathbb{O},
$$
and the **right alternative law**:
$$
x \cdot (y \cdot y) = (x \cdot y) \cdot y \quad \text{for all } x, y \in \mathbb{O}.
$$

Equivalently, defining the **associator** $[x, y, z] := (x \cdot y) \cdot z - x \cdot (y \cdot z)$, alternativity states:
$$
[x, x, y] = 0 \quad \text{and} \quad [x, y, y] = 0 \quad \text{for all } x, y \in \mathbb{O}.
$$

A corollary (the **flexible law**) is $[x, y, x] = 0$, i.e., $(x \cdot y) \cdot x = x \cdot (y \cdot x)$.

## Key Proof Steps

- **Basis reduction:** It suffices to check alternativity on basis elements $e_i$, since the laws are multilinear (degree 2 in the repeated variable) and the basis spans $\mathbb{O}$.
- **Imaginary squared:** For any imaginary basis unit $e_i$ with $i \geq 1$, we have $e_i^2 = -1$. Therefore $(e_i \cdot e_i) \cdot e_j = -e_j$. Separately, $e_i \cdot (e_i \cdot e_j)$: if $e_i \cdot e_j = \pm e_k$ then $e_i \cdot e_k$ is read from the Fano table and equals $\mp e_j$. Hence both sides equal $-e_j$.
- **Fano triple consistency:** The key algebraic fact is: for any Fano triple $(e_i, e_j, e_k)$, we have $e_i \cdot e_j = e_k$ and $e_i \cdot e_k = -e_j$. This means $e_i \cdot (e_i \cdot e_j) = e_i \cdot e_k = -e_j = e_i^2 \cdot e_j$. The Lean proof verifies this for all 7 triples by `decide`.
- **Decidable finite check:** Since both laws are bilinear (quadratic in the repeated variable) and the basis is finite (`Fin 8`), the check over all $8 \times 8$ basis pairs is decidable and verified computationally in `CausalGraphTheory/Octonions.lean`.
- **Artin's theorem (consequence):** Alternativity implies that every subalgebra generated by at most 2 elements is associative. This is used in ALG-003: the non-associativity witness requires *three* generators.

## Implications for COG

- **Power-associativity:** Alternativity implies $x^n$ is well-defined (bracketing-independent) for all $x \in \mathbb{O}$ and $n \in \mathbb{N}$, enabling consistent iteration in the discrete causal update rules.
- **Moufang identities:** Alternativity implies the three Moufang identities, which are used in DIST-001 (distributivity of the causal update) and in verifying the loop structure of the multiplicative group.
- **Gate overhead (TICK-001, MASS-001):** The distinction between the *associative* subalgebra (where alternativity is automatic) and the *full* non-associative octonion algebra is precisely what creates the $k_{\text{gate}} = 21$ overhead in TICK-001.
- **Norm multiplicativity (ALG-004):** The proof of $|x \cdot y| = |x| \cdot |y|$ uses the flexible law $[x, y, x] = 0$ as a key intermediate step.
