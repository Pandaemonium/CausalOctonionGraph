# The Electron's 4-Cycle: The Four-Stroke Engine of Reality

*An explanation for bright high schoolers and college students.*

Imagine a standard car engine. To keep the car moving forward, the engine has to constantly repeat a specific cycle: Intake, Compression, Power, and Exhaust. It takes exactly four distinct steps (or "strokes") for the piston to return to its exact starting position and begin the process all over again.

In **Causal Graph Theory**, we have discovered that the fundamental particles of the universe—like the electron—operate a lot like a microscopic, four-stroke engine.

## The Problem: What Does an Electron "Do"?

When we draw diagrams of particles interacting (like an electron emitting or absorbing a photon of light), we usually draw them as simple dots or lines. 

But if the universe is running on underlying mathematical rules, an electron isn't just a dot; it's a specific mathematical state. And when it interacts with a photon, that state has to change. The photon "kicks" the electron, mathematically speaking, updating its properties.

If an electron's state keeps getting kicked and updated every time it interacts with the vacuum of space, how does it stay an electron? Why doesn't it scramble into random mathematical noise? It must have a cycle—a rhythm that always brings it back home.

## What We Proved

Using our theorem-proving software, Lean 4, and extensive Python simulations, we analyzed what happens to the mathematical state of an electron when it interacts with the background vacuum (which we represent mathematically as multiplying the electron's state by the vacuum's state, $e_7$).

We proved the **Universal $C_e = 4$ Theorem**. 

Here is what it means:
1. Start with the electron in its base state.
2. Hit it with a photon (interact with the vacuum). The math changes the electron into a completely different intermediate state. 
3. Hit it again. It changes into a third state.
4. Hit it a third time. It changes into a fourth state.
5. **Hit it a fourth time, and the math perfectly cancels out all the changes, returning the electron to its exact original starting state.**

We proved this is true no matter what state you start in. Mathematically, the operation of "interacting with the vacuum" has a strict period of 4.

### Why is this a big deal?

1. **A Digital Pulse:** This result shows that the electron isn't just a static ball of charge. It has an internal "clock." It throbs or pulses through a four-step cycle as it moves through space.
2. **Mathematical Perfection:** We proved this algebraically. There is no fuzziness or approximation. The structure of the unique number system we use (the octonions) guarantees that four interactions *must* return you to the start, just like turning 90 degrees four times means you are facing forward again.
3. **A Surprising Twist on Mass:** Here is something our proof revealed that we did *not* expect. The muon — the electron's heavy sibling, about 207 times more massive — goes through *exactly the same four-step cycle*. The period is 4 for every particle, not just the electron. So where does the mass difference come from? Not the length of the cycle, but the *cost per step*. Absorbing a photon costs the muon 15 times the effort it costs the electron. Same four laps around the track, but the muon is running in concrete boots. That friction — the extra computational overhead per interaction — is what we experience as mass.

Think of it this way: the electron's state is like an arrow on a compass. Each photon rotates it 90°. North → East → South → West → North. Four rotations, back to the start. The muon's arrow also rotates exactly 90° per photon — but the muon lives on a stickier surface where each rotation costs 15 times the effort. Same geometry, different friction. That friction is mass.

Just like a car engine needs a four-stroke cycle to run reliably, the universe uses a strict four-step mathematical rhythm to keep particles stable — and the differences in how much *fuel* each stroke takes is what gives particles their different masses.
