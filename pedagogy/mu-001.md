# MU-001: The Proton-Electron Mass Ratio & The Associativity Gap

**Claim:** [MU-001]
**Status:** Revised Pending (Degenerate Result)
**Simulation:** `calc/mass_drag_v2.py`

## 1. Intuitive Motivation

One of the great mysteries of the Standard Model is the specific value of the proton-to-electron mass ratio, $\mu \approx 1836.15$. Why is the proton so much heavier than the electron?

In the Causal Octonion Graph (COG) framework, we treat physical observables as combinatorial properties of the graph evolution. "Mass" is hypothesized to be a form of **computational resistance** or **update overhead**. As the graph evolves, particles that require complex algebraic bookkeeping to maintain their coherence "tick" slower relative to the background causal flow. This retardation manifests physically as inertia, or mass.

The goal of MU-001 was to derive this ratio $\mu$ by comparing the update frequency of a Proton motif versus an Electron motif.

## 2. The Hypothesis: Pure Gate Density

The initial hypothesis (defined in RFC-009) was that mass is directly proportional to the density of "Gates" encountered during evolution.

A **Gate** in the COG model is defined as a non-associative boundary check. The octonions are non-associative, meaning $(ab)c \neq a(bc)$ in general. When the graph updates, if the local neighborhood forms a non-associative triad, the "Alternativity Trigger" fires, representing a cost or "gate" that must be processed.

**Hypothesis:** $\mu \approx \frac{\text{Gate Density(Proton)}}{\text{Gate Density(Electron)}}$

We expected the Proton (composed of quarks with color charge) to have a high gate density, and the Electron to have a low, non-zero density.

## 3. The Experiment

We implemented a simulation `calc/mass_drag_v2.py` to test this.

1.  **Proton Motif:** Modeled as three quarks exchanging gluons in a cycle.
    *   **State:** The colors are initialized to $\{e_1, e_2, e_4\}$.
    *   **Algebra:** These three imaginary units do **not** lie on the same Fano line. They generate the full Octonion algebra and form a non-associative triad.

2.  **Electron Motif:** Modeled as a lepton state cycling through its components.
    *   **State:** The components are initialized to $\{e_1, e_2, e_3\}$.
    *   **Algebra:** These three units lie on the "quaternion line" (or generate the quaternion subalgebra $\mathbb{H} \subset \mathbb{O}$).

The simulation ran for $N=10,000$ ticks, counting every time the associator $[s, g, t] = (s \cdot g) \cdot t - s \cdot (g \cdot t)$ was non-zero.

## 4. The Result: Degeneracy

The results were stark and immediate:

| Particle | Motif | Gate Density |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Proton** | $\{e_1, e_2, e_4\}$ | **1.0** (100%) |
| **Electron** | $\{e_1, e_2, e_3\}$ | **0.0** (0%) |

**Why is the Electron density 0.0?**
The units $\{e_1, e_2, e_3\}$ behave exactly like the imaginary units $i, j, k$ of the Quaternions. Quaternions are **associative**. Therefore, the error term $(ab)c - a(bc)$ is identically zero for every possible interaction within this motif. The electron never triggers the non-associativity check.

**Why is the Proton density 1.0?**
The units $\{e_1, e_2, e_4\}$ are maximally non-associative. Every step in the cycle involves a "turning" operation that fails associativity, requiring a gate correction every single tick.

## 5. Interpretation

The calculated ratio is:
$$ \mu = \frac{1.0}{0.0} \to \infty $$

This result is **degenerate**. It implies that if mass were purely "gate density," the electron would be massless (like a photon), or the proton infinitely massive relative to it.

However, this "failure" is a significant theoretical success in disguise. It **falsifies** the naive "Pure Gate Density" model but **confirms** a fundamental algebraic distinction:

*   **Leptons** reside in the **Associative** sector ($\mathbb{H} \subset \mathbb{O}$). They slide through the graph with minimal algebraic friction.
*   **Baryons** reside in the **Non-Associative** sector ($\mathbb{O} \setminus \mathbb{H}$). They are structurally "gritty" and incur heavy computational costs.

The experiment proves that the "Associativity Gap" is real and binary (0 vs 1) in this simplified limit.

## 6. The Path Forward: Tick Overhead

To resolve the degeneracy and recover a finite mass ratio, we moved to the **Tick Overhead** model (detailed in RFC-034 and `pedagogy/tick-001.md`).

We now assign a **baseline cost** of 1 tick for any state update (associative or not), representing the basic flow of causality. The non-associative "gate" adds a penalty $k$.

*   **Electron Mass:** $m_e \propto 1 \text{ (baseline)} + 0 \text{ (penalty)} = 1$
*   **Proton Mass:** $m_p \propto 1 \text{ (baseline)} + k \times 1.0 \text{ (penalty)} = 1 + k$

The ratio becomes $\mu = 1 + k$. The theoretical task is now to derive $k$ from the size of the automorphism groups involved (e.g., $|GL(3,2)| = 168$ vs Stabilizer sizes), aiming for $k \approx 1835$.
