# Discovering Integer Triples in Koide's Formula

## What Was Proved
In this project, we conducted an exhaustive search for integer triples that satisfy a peculiar mathematical condition known as the Koide formula. Our focus was on finding sets of three integers between 1 and 4000 whose Koide ratio was approximately \( \frac{2}{3} \). This criterion was successfully met by several triples, providing concrete examples that fit within the specified bounds.

## How It Works
The Koide formula is a mathematical expression given by:

$$ R(f_1, f_2, f_3) = \frac{(f_1 + f_2 + f_3)^2}{3(f_1^2 + f_2^2 + f_3^2)} $$

which we aimed to evaluate for integer values $f_1, f_2, f_3$. The goal was to find integer sets for which the ratio \( R(f_1, f_2, f_3) \) closely approximates \(\frac{2}{3}\). We specifically conducted a search over the domain where \(1 \leq f_1 \leq f_2 \leq f_3 \leq 4000\). Extensive iterations allowed us to gather these triples, with the best matches sorted by their closeness to the target value of the ratio.
